This project works with volunteers across Germany to record insects using car nets to assess the impact of land use intensity and fragmentation of insect populations. DNA-metabarcoding and automated image classification methods are developed with partners to aid insect identification. In addition, motivation in conservation-based citizen science was analyzed.

Insects from five different landscapes were sampled, which include forests, urban areas, agricultural lands, webland and grassland. Citizens will drive car with nets on top through the planned routes through these landscapes every year in June or July. Sampling will be done twice per day for the same route, one during noon time, another later in the afternoon. The insect samples will first be pre-sorted and recorded in the laboratory using automated image classification, and then sequenced using metabarcoding to determine biomass, abundance and species composition using modern methods.

The aim:
to determine the occurrence of insects and their biomass, abundance (number) and species composition in the landscape.
